\textbf{Re: Submission of manuscript ``Linear response representation of two-fermion plasmas''}

\vspace{0.5em}
Dear Editor,

We submit the accompanying manuscript for consideration as a Regular Article in \textit{Physical Review E}.
The paper develops a causality-constrained, deterministic framework for finite-temperature Lindhard linear response and two-component random-phase approximation (RPA) dielectric functions in homogeneous, isotropic electron--ion plasmas.

\textbf{Context and significance.}
Accurate evaluation of $\varepsilon(q,\omega)$ and the dynamic structure factors $S_{st}(q,\omega)$ under partial degeneracy is central to plasma spectroscopy, screening theory, and the interpretation of collective modes such as plasmons and Landau damping.
For decades, finite-temperature Lindhard response has been approached through contour integration and Matsubara-like pole summations.
We show that widely used hybrid routes---in which the imaginary part is obtained in a grand-canonical prescription while the real part is assembled from canonical contour sums or mismatched analytic continuations---violate the Kramers--Kr\"onig relation and produce $\mathcal{O}(1)$ errors in $\Re\chi^L$ that persist in the highly degenerate $T \to 0$ limit.
These failures are not numerical accidents; they reflect an ensemble inconsistency that breaks exact causality.

\textbf{Main results.}
The manuscript maps the calculation into a strictly constrained physical space: $\Im\chi^L$ is evaluated in closed form within the grand-canonical ensemble, and $\Re\chi^L$ is obtained \emph{exclusively} through the Kramers--Kr\"onig transform of that causal dissipative part, bypassing divergent Matsubara pole summations via a strictly causal real-axis formulation.
The Hilbert transform is implemented with a modified sinc-quadrature rule whose theoretical truncation error bound is $\mathcal{O}(10^{-259})$ under the parameter choice reported in the manuscript---anchoring the evaluation well below double-precision limits while preserving causality down to machine precision.

We validate the SOPHUS-Lindhard implementation through deterministic extraction of electron plasmon dispersion and Landau damping, automated verification of four macroscopic dynamic structure factor sum rules within machine precision, and a highly degenerate limit test that isolates the ensemble inconsistency of legacy contour-based presentations.
The same pinned pipeline extends to the full two-component RPA susceptibility tensor and to $S_{ee}$, $S_{ii}$, and $S_{ei}$ without ad hoc regularization or parameter tuning.

\textbf{Appropriateness for \textit{Physical Review E}.}
The work addresses causality, spectral representation, and thermodynamic consistency in partially degenerate Coulomb plasmas---a core subject area of \textit{Physical Review E}.
We believe the combination of analytic diagnosis, deterministic numerics, and macroscopic sum-rule validation will interest readers working on plasma dielectric functions, RPA-based screening, and finite-temperature Fermi systems.

It is not part of a joint submission.
